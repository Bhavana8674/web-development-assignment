  • Here's a timeline outlining some significant events in the life of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar:

    • 1891 (April 14):Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was born in Mhow, in the Central Provinces (now in Madhya Pradesh), India.
    • 1907-1912:He pursued his education at Elphinstone College in Bombay (now Mumbai), and later at the University of Bombay (now University of Mumbai).
    • 1913:Ambedkar received a scholarship from the Gaekwar of Baroda, which enabled him to study at Columbia University in New York City.
    • 1916: He completed his M.A. and Ph.D. in Economics from Columbia University, becoming one of the first Indians to earn a doctorate in economics abroad.
    • 1917:Ambedkar was awarded a D.Sc. degree by the University of London for his thesis on the "Problem of the Rupee: Its Origin and Its Solution".
    • 1920s: He emerged as a prominent leader advocating for the rights of Dalits (formerly known as untouchables) in India. He founded several organizations to champion their cause.
    • 1932:Ambedkar played a crucial role in the Round Table Conferences, where he advocated for the rights of the Dalit community.
    • 1935: He was appointed as the principal of the Government Law College in Mumbai.
    • 1936: Ambedkar formed the Independent Labour Party, which aimed to address the issues faced by the Dalit community and other marginalized groups.
    • 1947: After India gained independence from British rule, Ambedkar was appointed as the nation's first Law Minister in the Cabinet of Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru.
    • 1949:He played a pivotal role in drafting the Indian Constitution, which was adopted on January 26, 1950.
    • 1956:Ambedkar converted to Buddhism along with thousands of his followers, as a form of protest against the discrimination faced by Dalits within the Hindu caste system. This event is known as the "Dhamma Chakra Pravartan Din".
    • 1956: (December 6):** Dr. B.R. Ambedkar passed away in Delhi.
    • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's legacy continues to inspire social justice movements in India and around the world. He is widely revered as the chief architect of the Indian Constitution and a champion of the rights of marginalized communities.

    “Life should be great rather than long.” ― Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar
